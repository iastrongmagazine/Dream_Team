# Multi-Team Coordination

## When to Use

This guide applies when coordinating across multiple teams or stakeholders.

## Coordination Patterns

### Pattern 1: Standalone Sync

**Use when**: Single team, regular cadence.

```
Weekly sync:
- 15 min max
- Status: What did we do? What's next?
- Blockers: What needs help?
- Decisions: What needs approval?
```

### Pattern 2: Cross-Functional Alignment

**Use when**: Multiple teams need to align on shared goals.

```
Preparation:
1. Collect updates from each team
2. Identify dependencies and conflicts
3. Prepare agenda with time allocations

Meeting Structure:
- 5 min: Context (why we're here)
- 20 min: Updates (one team at a time, max 2 min each)
- 15 min: Conflicts (what needs resolution)
- 10 min: Next steps (who does what by when)

Follow-up:
- Send summary within 2 hours
- Track cross-team dependencies
- Escalate blockers early
```

### Pattern 3: Executive Briefing

**Use when**: Reporting to leadership or board.

```
Format:
- One page max
- Highlights (3 items max)
- Decisions needed
- Risks and mitigations
- Metrics snapshot

Timing:
- Send 24 hours before
- Follow up day-of
- Keep under 10 minutes
```

### Pattern 4: Stakeholder Management

**Use when**: Managing expectations with external stakeholders.

```
Key Principles:
1. Set expectations early (timeline, deliverables)
2. Communicate proactively (don't wait for them to ask)
3. Document everything (decisions, agreements)
4. Escalate early (before issues become crises)

Update Template:
- What we accomplished
- What's next
- What we need from them
- Timeline update (if any)
```
