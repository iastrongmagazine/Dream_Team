# Competitive Analysis

## Framework

### 1. Identify Competitors

**Direct competitors**: Same product, same customers
**Indirect competitors**: Different product, same customers
**Alternative competitors**: Same job, different solution

### 2. Research Sources

- Website and marketing materials
- Product demos and trials
- Review sites (G2, Capterra)
- Social media
- Press and news
- Customer interviews

### 3. Analysis Dimensions

| Dimension | What to Look For |
|-----------|------------------|
| Positioning | How do they position themselves? |
| Product | Features, UX, quality |
| Pricing | Model, tiers, discounts |
| Go-to-market | Channels, messages, campaigns |
| Customers | Who do they serve? |
| Strengths | What do they do well? |
| Weaknesses | Where are they vulnerable? |

### 4. Competitive Matrix

```
| Feature           | Us    | Comp A | Comp B |
|-------------------|-------|--------|--------|
| Feature 1         | ✅    | ✅     | ❌     |
| Feature 2         | ✅    | ❌     | ✅     |
| Price             | $$    | $$$    | $      |
```

## Competitive Response

### When competitor launches something new

1. **Assess**: Is it truly innovative or me-too?
2. **Analyze**: How does it affect our positioning?
3. **Decide**: Ignore / Match / Differentiate
4. **Communicate**: Tell your team and customers

### When competitor drops price

1. **Understand**: Why did they drop price?
2. **Evaluate**: Impact on our business
3. **Decide**: Match / Value-add instead / Stay firm
4. **Message**: Focus on value, not price

### When competitor wins a deal

1. **Learn**: What did they offer that we didn't?
2. **Analyze**: Is this a pattern or one-off?
3. **Improve**: Update positioning if needed
4. **Address**: Close gaps if legitimate
