# Team Management

## Marketing Team Structure

### Early-Stage Startup (1-5 people)

Typical structure:
- Head of Marketing (you)
- Maybe one other: Content OR Growth

**Focus**: Get traction, prove channels

### Growth Stage (5-15 people)

Typical structure:
- Head of Marketing
- Demand Gen / Growth
- Content / Brand
- Product Marketing
- Operations / Analytics

**Focus**: Scale what works

### Scale-Up (15-50 people)

Typical structure:
- CMO
- VP Demand Gen
- VP Brand / Content
- VP Product Marketing
- Marketing Ops
- Analytics

**Focus**: Efficiency, measurement

### Interview Process

1. **Screen** (30 min): Fit and basics
2. **Assignment** (2-4 hours): Work sample
3. **Deep Dive** (45 min): Experience and approach
4. **Team Fit** (30 min): With team members
5. **Final** (30 min): With leadership

### Onboarding Checklist

```
Week 1:
[ ] Set up tools and access
[ ] Review company overview
[ ] Meet team members
[ ] Review marketing strategy
[ ] Understand product

Week 2:
[ ] Shadow key processes
[ ] Take on first project
[ ] Meet with stakeholders
[ ] Set 30-day goals

Week 3-4:
[ ] Execute independently
[ ] Get feedback
[ ] Adjust approach
```

## Performance Management

### Setting Goals (OKR Framework)

**Objectives** (What we want to achieve):
- Qualitative
- Ambitious
- Time-bound

**Key Results** (How we'll measure it):
- Quantitative
- Measurable
- Verifyable

Example:
- **O**: Become the leading solution in our category
- **KR1**: 50% brand awareness (from 20%)
- **KR2**: 100 MQLs/month (from 40)
- **KR3**: 30% market share in target segment

### Giving Feedback

**Feedback Framework**:
1. **Observation**: What I observed
2. **Impact**: The impact it had
3. **Expectation**: What I expect going forward
4. **Support**: How I can help

### Performance Review

**Quarterly Review**:
- Progress on goals
- Strengths and areas for growth
- Career development
- Compensation decisions
